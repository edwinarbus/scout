  • Adoption Pending – When an animal has become available after the legal holding period but hasn’t gone through S/N and a potential adopter has been given a specific date and time for pick up. The only requirement for the potential adopter is to be present at the Animal Care Center at the time of expressing interest.
  • Stray wait – Animals found without identification not yet available pending the state-mandated holding period.
  • Ready to go Home/RTGH – Animals ready for adoption and available to go home the same day.
  • Available Pending Spay/Neuter (AV Pend SN)– An animal that has completed its legal holding period and in now awaiting spay or neuter surgery.
  • Pending Assessment (AV BET) – An animal who is pending a behavior assessment prior to becoming available.
  • PDD (Potentially Dangerous Dog) –A dog that is being held pending the outcome of a dangerous dog investigation.
  • Quarantine – An animal that has bitten a person, or bit or was bitten by other animal and is being held for the State-mandated quarantine period. Quarantine periods may vary depending on the circumstances of the incident.
  • Rescue Only – An animal that can only be adopted by a department approved Adoption Partner or authorized rescue organization due to behavior or medical reasons.
  • ID Hold – Any animal which has any identification (license tag, personal tag, microchip).
  • Other Hold – Animals not available for other reasons.

*Animal status can be subject to change at any time depending on each animals’ circumstances.

**The more filters you apply, the fewer animals may appear in your search results.

When Surrender Feels Like the Only Option – DACC Is Here to Help (No Appointment Needed)

At the Los Angeles County Department of Animal Care and Control (DACC), we understand that circumstances can change, and sometimes people feel they have no choice but to give up their pet. While we no longer require appointments to surrender, we strongly encourage you to explore resources that may help you keep your pet at home, rehome them, or connect with a local rescue organization. These options often give pets the best chance at a smooth transition and reduce stress for both you and your pet.

Please take a moment to review these helpful options before making the decision to surrender: https://animalcare.lacounty.gov/rehome-your-pet/

If you’ve explored all other options and surrendering is the best path forward, we’re here to provide compassionate care throughout the process. No appointment is needed — just visit your local DACC Animal Care Center during business hours. Our goal is to support both you and your pet every step of the way.

Thank you for taking the time to consider all options for your pet’s future.
